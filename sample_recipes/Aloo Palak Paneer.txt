1. **Aloo Palak Paneer** 
2. **Preparation Time**: 15 minutes
3. **Cooking Time**: 30 minutes
4. **Number of Servings**: 2
5. **Ingredients**:
   - 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into small cubes
   - 200g paneer, cut into small cubes
   - 2 tablespoons ghee
   - 1 cup tightly packed chopped palak (spinach) leaves
   - 1 medium onion, finely chopped
   - 2 garlic cloves, minced
   - 1 teaspoon grated ginger
   - 1/2 teaspoon cumin powder
   - 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
   - 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
   - 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
   - 1/2 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
   - 1/4 teaspoon sugar (optional)
   - A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
   - Water as needed
   - Freshly ground black pepper to taste

6. **Instructions**:
   1. Heat 1 tablespoon of ghee in a pan over medium heat. Add the cubed potatoes and fry until they are lightly browned and almost cooked through. Remove from the pan and set aside.
   2. In the same pan, heat the remaining ghee. Add the asafoetida, followed by the chopped onions. Sauté until the onions turn translucent. 
   3. Add the ginger and garlic, sauté for a minute, and then add the cumin, coriander, and turmeric powders. Cook for about 2 minutes, stirring frequently. 
   4. Now, add the chopped spinach and continue cooking until it wilts and reduces in volume. 
   5. Transfer the mixture to a blender and blend into a smooth paste, adding very little water if needed. 
   6. Pour the blended paste back into the pan and add the fried potatoes. Stir well and add the chili powder, salt, and sugar (if using). 
   7. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally. Then, add the paneer cubes and gently mix them into the gravy. Cook for another 2-3 minutes. 
   8. Check for seasoning and adjust salt and pepper as needed. 

7. **Serving**: 
Serve Aloo Palak Paneer hot with roti, naan, or jeera rice for a delicious and satisfying Indian meal. 

Enjoy your homemade Indian feast!
